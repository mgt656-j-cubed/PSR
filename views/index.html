Burn Down Chart

All story points (and corresponding stories) burned down!

Sprint Completed Stories

The following stories have been completed since last sprint:

  • Users can now view the About page
  • Users can now see details of events in their own pages
  • Users can see event title, image, location, date and time, and attendees
  • Users can submit their email for RSVP and sites validates if it's a Yale address or not
  • Admin can track user flow through Google Analytics
  • RSVP experiment set-up
  • Donate/Support experiment set-up

We had a hard time figuring out the Google Analytics portion of the project despite our initial belief that it was going to be easy. Finally got it to work after having seen instructors do it in class.

That said, we are hesitant to make conclusions based on our experiments given the short time that they have been running.

Our initial plans to devote the last sprint to making the site more aesthetically pleasing had to be put on the back burner, which is unfortunate because it was the part of the project that we were most excited about.

Product Backlog

The following stories are scheduled for development in the future:

  • Continue run of existing experiments to gather more data on RSVP and Donate/Support variants
  • Implement experiments variants that lead to higher conversion

All of these things are dependent on whether the simulated traffic will keep running while the site is up. We will probably also try and crack the CSS challenge and find inspiration from some of the work that we have seen in class.

Project Retrospective

The project has been a great learning experience and these are some of the things we learned:

  • Constant communication is key.
  • As we have learned in class, building the capabilities of the team should take precedence over checking off product requirements.
  • Never, ever make the mistake of saying "it's just a few lines of code" especially when you don't even have an idea of what the code would look like.
Traffic Analysis

Based on our experiments, we have found the following to be optimal for user conversion:

  • Having RSVP in the events list increases (slightly) the number of pageviews. Having RSVP increased pageviews by 1% versus original.
  • The word Donate works better than Support in converting users from viewers to donors/supporters. Support variant led to 37% lower conversion versus Donate variant.
